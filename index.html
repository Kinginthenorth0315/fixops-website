HubSpot doesn't tell you when things go wrong. No alerts for silent failures. No warning before you hit your billing tier. No flag when a dashboard starts pulling corrupt data. That's what FixOps is for.

Workflows failing silently

HubSpot marks a workflow "Active" even when actions are failing. Leads disappear. Reps don't get tasks. Nobody finds out until someone complains. This is the #1 revenue leak we find every audit.

HubSpot's native dedup only catches exact email matches. Fuzzy duplicates — same person, different formats — pile up and push you into the next contact tier every month.

⚡ Portal Audit

210 checks. Every corner of your portal. Plain English results.

FixOps connects via read-only OAuth and runs a 210-point audit across 8 health dimensions — Data Integrity, Automation Health, Pipeline Integrity, Configuration, Marketing Health, Reporting Quality, Service Health, and Team Adoption. Every issue includes a dollar impact estimate, a root-cause description, and a numbered fix guide you can act on immediately.

Not a 139-page PDF. Expandable issue cards. Step-by-step fix guides and a Fix It For Me button on every finding.

What we actually read via API: contacts, companies, deals, tickets, quotes, invoices, subscriptions, workflows, sequences, forms, lists, email campaigns, meeting links, knowledge base articles, custom objects, users, teams, and portal settings. All read-only. We never write, create, or modify anything in your portal. Revoke access instantly from HubSpot Settings → Connected Apps.

🔀 Workflow Conflict Detector

Two workflows. Same trigger. Nobody noticed.

The most expensive HubSpot mistake nobody talks about: two active workflows enrolling the same contacts from the same form submission, sending duplicate emails for months until someone unsubscribes.

FixOps maps every active workflow trigger and flags any pair that will co-enroll contacts. Duplicate form triggers, parallel lifecycle workflows, multiple versions running simultaneously — all caught automatically.

What we detect: Duplicate form submission triggers, overlapping lifecycle stage triggers, parallel active workflow versions with similar names, and conflicting enrollment conditions.
